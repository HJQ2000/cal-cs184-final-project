In project 3-1 and 3-2 we implement pathtracing rendering on glass, mirror and microfacet material, but do not have support for texture. We want to add texture support to make the rendering more appealing and interesting.

Our goal is to render objects with more varied and realistic looking. We want to render objects with different surface texture.

We want to keep the rendering fast, reusable, and can cope with different texture realistically. We want to see a rabbit wearing cloth.

More complicated texture involving more complex reflection and refraction. Finding suitablt refletion and refraction is one of the main point.

As in project 1, we see texture mapping in 2D. Here we want to accomplish texture mapping in 3D. We think the overall logic will be similar.


Our initial goal is to have a rabbit wearing realistic cloth (including pleats and yarn) or make the rabbit looks hairy. We then realize that it may not be possible soly by texture rendering. If we want to have pleats and shapes outside of the main object, things may go very different.

We will deliver sphere with different texture, and rabbit in tight cloth.
